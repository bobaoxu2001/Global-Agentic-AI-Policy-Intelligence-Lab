## Portfolio-level recommendation

Approve investment in **reusable controls and evidence collection**, not a universal launch decision. The limited Atlas corpus supports a global architecture baseline—identity, scoped authority, logs, disclosure, approval gates, testing, and redress—but it does not establish that one configuration satisfies every law, sector, or market. Each launch still requires an up-to-date, product-specific, jurisdiction-specific review.

## Opportunity lens

This matrix treats policy as a design constraint and a market signal:

- **Standards participation** can turn internal agent-control experience into interoperable ecosystem influence.
- **Assurance evidence** can reduce enterprise adoption friction.
- **Disclosure and redress** can differentiate consumer trust rather than merely satisfy a minimum.
- **Infrastructure policy** can open capacity and partnership options while simultaneously requiring more deliberate sovereignty and export-control architecture.
