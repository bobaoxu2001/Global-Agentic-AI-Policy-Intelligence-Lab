# Executive Decision Brief — Global Agentic AI Launch Readiness

**As of:** 2026-07-14
**Status:** final independent portfolio work sample; underlying structured policy records remain `in_review`
**Decision owner:** hypothetical global AI leadership team
**Boundary:** AI-assisted research; not legal advice, a company recommendation, or launch authorization

## Decision

**Do not approve one universal market launch from this limited preview. Approve the reusable control layer and require a product-specific, market-specific decision after current primary law, operating evidence, and qualified review are complete.**

## Why this is the defensible call

| Market | Source-backed signal | Evidence | Business implication (analysis) | Decision now |

| **Singapore** | The 2026 agentic-AI framework and sandbox work provide non-binding governance and testing direction. | S08, S09 | Assurance evidence can reduce adoption friction without being treated as legal authorization. | Crosswalk controls and nominate a low-risk, evidence-ready sandbox use case. |

## Material risks and opportunities

- **Risk:** a high-risk delay is misread as a general transparency delay.
- **Risk:** sparse coverage or a low heuristic score is treated as evidence that no duty applies.
- **Risk:** permanent authority, supplier, customer, or regional commitments close policy options too early.
- **Opportunity:** identity, least privilege, logs, approval, disclosure, testing, and redress become shared platform capabilities.
- **Opportunity:** assurance and standards participation can turn implementation evidence into trust and interoperability advantages.

## 30-day operating plan

1. **Policy — Day 5:** refresh the applicability screen; record status, bindingness, dates, source IDs, confidence, and the product fact creating the question.
2. **Product + Legal — Day 10:** classify each market configuration across direct interaction, generated content, consequential decisions, profiling, minors, sectors, and distribution.
3. **Engineering + Security — Day 21:** implement and test expiring authority, allowlisted tools, logs, approval thresholds, kill switch, disclosure, and redress.
4. **Leadership — Day 30:** review evidence, sensitivity, challenged assumptions, unresolved questions, owners, and re-verification triggers before any market decision.

## Five launch gates

1. System and capability profile confirmed.
2. Current primary sources checked against the exact product facts.
3. Controls tested in the real workflow, not merely planned.
4. Sensitivity and unresolved questions shown.
5. Qualified human review and accountable decision recorded.
