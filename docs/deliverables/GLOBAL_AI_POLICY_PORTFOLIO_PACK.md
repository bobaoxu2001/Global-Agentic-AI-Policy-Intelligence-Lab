**Status:** independent, AI-assisted portfolio research; not legal advice; no company affiliation

## Executive readout

Policy is moving on two connected tracks. Infrastructure policy can widen compute capacity and commercial routes, while product rules and standards raise expectations for identity, authorization, disclosure, human accountability, auditability, and redress. A global operator should treat these as one product-and-market planning problem without flattening different legal regimes into one answer.

**Recommended decision:** fund a reusable control layer now—identity, scoped authority, action evidence, disclosure and provenance, approval thresholds, testing, rollback, and redress. Hold any universal launch decision until the relevant product facts, current primary law, operating evidence, and qualified market-specific review are available.

This is a reversible operating recommendation, not a legal conclusion or launch authorization.

## Decision architecture

### Approve now

1. **Shared control layer**
   - short-lived identity and scoped authorization;
   - deny-by-default tool access;
   - immutable action logs and named decision owners;
   - first-interaction disclosure and output provenance;
   - approval thresholds, safe stop, rollback, appeal, and redress.
2. **Thirty-day evidence cycle**
   - days 0–7: product-market inventory, named policy/legal owners, and dated re-verification triggers;
   - days 8–20: disclosure/redress UX tests, identity and log evidence, export and ownership screens;
   - days 21–30: market launch memo, cross-functional evidence review, and standards or sandbox pack.

### Require before each launch

- the exact product journey, deployment model, user group, sector, data flow, model or agent authority, and distribution channel;
- current authoritative law and official guidance for the selected market;
- evidence that the selected controls operate as intended;
- qualified human review and an explicit owner decision.

### Do not infer

- that one control configuration is lawful everywhere;
- that voluntary guidance creates legal authorization;
- that a delayed deadline is a general pause;
- that a high prioritization score proves legal exposure;
- that a portfolio sample represents an external filing, company position, stakeholder relationship, or internal outcome.

## Public-affairs operating samples

This pack includes four different external-policy work modes. Each is deliberately labeled as a sample.

1. **Policy position:** `POLICY_POSITION_NOTE_SAMPLE.md` develops outcome-based asks for secure and interoperable agents, including trade-offs, red lines, and stakeholder questions.
2. **Consultation response:** `CONSULTATION_RESPONSE_SAMPLE.md` turns the position into regulator-facing answers, implementation evidence, safeguards, and bounded requests. It was not submitted.
3. **Speaking brief:** `SPEAKING_BRIEF_SAMPLE.md` provides an opening, message house, proof points, anticipated questions, bridges, and red lines. No invitation or speaking engagement is claimed.
4. **Meeting preparation:** `MEETING_BRIEF_SAMPLE.md` and `STAKEHOLDER_LANDSCAPE.json` connect objective, messages, questions, leave-behind, follow-up, and relationship status. Every relationship is marked `not_contacted`.

`INDUSTRY_INITIATIVES_TRACKER.md` adds a source-dated operating view of standards, coalitions, assurance programs, participation options, owners, and next-review triggers. It does not claim membership or participation.

## Quantitative discipline

The application includes an Agentic Deployment Risk Score (ADRS) to demonstrate transparent prioritization logic:

`ADRS = min(100, InherentRisk × (1 − MitigationCredit) × J)`

The score is an author-designed heuristic. It is not a probability, compliance score, legal determination, or externally calibrated measure. `ADRS_SENSITIVITY_NOTE.md` shows how anchor and component changes affect the result and defines appropriate decision use.

`POLICY_CORPUS_DESCRIPTIVE_ANALYSIS.md` is generated directly from the tracked JSON corpus. It reports counts, denominators, coverage ratios, source composition, freshness, and publication state. The small provision denominator and zero published records are shown rather than hidden.
